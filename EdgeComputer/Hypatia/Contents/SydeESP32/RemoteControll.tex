\subsection{Controller Module}
The controller is a handheld unit built around a third Hypatia board that provides real-time command and feedback for the External Force system. Its electronics, user controls, and phone mount are packaged in a compact front-panel enclosure so the operator can drive and monitor the demonstrator with one device.

The Hypatia board sits at the core and generates the motor commands and handles the wireless link. Two KY-023 joysticks \cite{JoyIT:2017} on the front panel map directly to the two drive motors: the left joystick commands the left motor and the right joystick the right motor. Pushing one joystick forward and one backward commands rotation. The joystick deflection is converted to a \ac{PWM} duty cycle for speed, while the sign of the deflection sets the motor polarity. A small display window labeled VELOCITY shows live telemetry received from the External Force system, namely the measured wheel speed, along with brief status text for connection. A momentary RECONNECT button is provided to re-initiate pairing and refresh the ESP-NOW peer configuration without cycling power. A latching ON/OFF switch removes system power when the unit is not in use (see figure (\ref{fig:Controller})).

The top of the remote controller features a slide-in rail for the phone attachment (see figure \ref{fig:PhoneStand}). To its left, a cable pass-through provides routing to the power bank mounted at the rear of the controller. The phone attachment includes a secondary sliding clamp that stabilizes the phone in position (see figure \ref{fig:PhoneHolder}). Both pieces are secured from the back with two wing nuts, allowing quick, tool-free adjustment.

The controller is supplied by a 5V power bank housed inside the Phone Attachment. The Hypatia board and the auxiliary electronics are fed from regulated rails derived from this source. The single front-panel switch disconnects the upstream supply so the entire controller can be shut down in one action.
